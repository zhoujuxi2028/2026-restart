# Day 9: Cross-cultural Collaboration + Agile/Scrum + Mock Interview

## Overview

Day 9 focuses on soft skills and team dynamics critical for the BASF role, plus a comprehensive mock interview to integrate all technical knowledge from Days 1-8. This day emphasizes working effectively in a distributed, cross-cultural team environment.

**Key Focus Areas:**
1. **Agile/Scrum Practices**: Sprint ceremonies, user stories, DoD/DoR
2. **Cross-cultural Team Collaboration**: Working with US, India, Europe colleagues
3. **Communication Skills**: Async tools, documentation, code reviews

5. **Full Mock Interview**: 60-minute simulation covering all topics

## Tips for Success

### Agile/Scrum
- **Think collaborative**: QA is not a gatekeeper, but an enabler of quality
- **Be specific**: Instead of "we test stories," say "I write acceptance criteria in refinement, automate tests during development, and validate in staging before sprint end"
- **Show flexibility**: Agile is about adapting—give examples of handling changes

### Cross-cultural Collaboration
- **Show cultural awareness**: Acknowledge time zone challenges and how you overcome them
- **Emphasize documentation**: Distributed teams rely on written communication
- **Demonstrate proactivity**: Don't wait for meetings—use async tools effectively

### Mock Interview
- **Treat it seriously**: Set up a professional environment, dress appropriately
- **Record yourself**: Video is best for body language review
- **Time strictly**: Practice answering within time limits (2-3 min per question)
- **Get feedback**: If possible, have a friend/colleague conduct the mock interview

---

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

❌ **Agile:**
- Saying "QA tests at the end of the sprint" (should be continuous)
- Not understanding DoD or acceptance criteria
- Viewing testing as separate from development (should be integrated)

❌ **Collaboration:**
- Only mentioning synchronous communication (meetings)
- Not addressing time zone challenges
- Lack of specific examples of documentation or async communication

❌ **Mock Interview:**
- Memorizing answers word-for-word (sounds robotic)
- Giving vague answers without examples or metrics
- Not preparing questions to ask the interviewer
- Rushing through answers or rambling

## Quick Reference

**Agile Ceremonies:**
- **Sprint Planning**: Define what to build and how
- **Daily Stand-up**: 15-min sync on progress/blockers
- **Refinement**: Clarify upcoming stories, define acceptance criteria
- **Sprint Review**: Demo completed work to stakeholders
- **Retrospective**: Team reflection on process improvements

**Cross-cultural Success Factors:**
- **Time zone overlap**: Schedule meetings during shared working hours
- **Async-first**: Don't block on meetings—use Slack, email, Confluence
- **Clear documentation**: README files, test reports, decision logs
- **Cultural sensitivity**: Adapt communication style, be patient with language differences

**Mock Interview Structure:**
- Part 1: Technical questions (20 min)
- Part 2: Behavioral/STAR stories (20 min)
- Part 3: Scenario-based problem-solving (10 min)
- Part 4: Your questions for interviewer (10 min)
