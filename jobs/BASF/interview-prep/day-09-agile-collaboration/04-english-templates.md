# Day 9: English Communication Templates - Agile + Collaboration

## Technical Vocabulary

### Agile/Scrum Terms
- **Sprint**: sprint (1-4 week development cycle)
- **Stand-up**: stand-up (daily 15-min sync meeting)
- **Retrospective**: ret-roh-spek-tiv (team reflection meeting)
- **Definition of Done**: dee-fi-nish-un of dun (DoD - completion criteria)
- **Acceptance Criteria**: ak-sep-tans cry-teer-ee-uh (story requirements)
- **Story Points**: story points (relative effort estimate)
- **Planning Poker**: plan-ing poh-ker (estimation technique)
- **Velocity**: vuh-lah-sih-tee (team's delivery pace)
- **Backlog**: bak-lawg (list of upcoming work)
- **Product Owner**: prah-dukt oh-ner (PO - business representative)

### Collaboration Terms
- **Asynchronous**: ay-sin-kruh-nus (not real-time, async)
- **Time Zone**: time zohn (geographic time difference)
- **Distributed Team**: dis-trib-yoo-ted teem (geographically spread)
- **Cross-functional**: cross-funk-shun-ul (diverse skills in one team)
- **Stakeholder**: stake-hohl-der (anyone with interest in project)
- **Confluence**: kon-floo-ens (documentation/wiki tool)
- **Pull Request**: pool ree-kwest (PR - code review request)

## STAR Format Stories

### Story: Agile Quality in Fast Sprints

**Situation**: "Our team adopted 2-week sprints, and initially, quality suffered because testing happened at the sprint end, causing last-minute bugs."

**Task**: "Integrate testing throughout the sprint to maintain quality without slowing delivery."

**Action**: "I proposed changes to our process: (1) I participated in sprint planning to define acceptance criteria upfront, ensuring stories were testable. (2) I wrote automated tests in parallel with development—not waiting for 'code complete.' (3) I conducted daily PR reviews, suggesting test coverage before merge. (4) We added 'automated tests written and passing' to our Definition of Done, making testing non-negotiable. (5) I ran exploratory testing mid-sprint to find integration issues early."

**Result**: "Defect leakage to production dropped from 12% to 2%. Sprint predictability improved—we hit 95% of sprint commitments vs 70% previously. Developers appreciated early feedback from automated tests catching bugs before manual testing. The team adopted this as our standard process."

### Story: Cross-cultural Team Collaboration

**Situation**: "I joined a team with members in San Francisco, London, and Bangalore—minimal overlapping working hours and cultural differences in communication styles."

**Task**: "Build effective collaboration despite time zones and cultural differences."

**Action**: "I implemented several strategies: (1) Identified a 2-hour daily overlap window for critical meetings. (2) Adopted async-first approach—posted daily updates in Slack so teammates saw progress overnight. (3) Documented everything in Confluence—test strategies, decisions, troubleshooting guides—enabling self-service. (4) Recorded all meetings for those who couldn't attend. (5) Adjusted communication style—I learned UK colleagues preferred formal emails, while US team liked quick Slack messages, so I adapted. (6) Scheduled 1-on-1s with each team member to build personal connections. (7) Organized virtual team events to build camaraderie."

**Result**: "Team collaboration scores in surveys improved from 3.2/5 to 4.6/5 over six months. We successfully delivered 8 sprints on time with high quality. Colleagues specifically appreciated my documentation—one said 'I can get unblocked at 2 AM without waiting for your response.' I learned that cultural intelligence and proactive communication are as important as technical skills."

## Interview Response Templates

### Template: Describing Agile Experience

1. **Role** (1 sentence): How do you participate?
2. **Ceremonies** (2-3 points): Specific contributions in each
3. **Value** (1-2 sentences): What impact do you have?
4. **Example** (30 seconds): Concrete story

**Example:**
"In Agile, I'm embedded in the cross-functional team, not a separate testing phase. [Role] I participate in sprint planning to define acceptance criteria and identify test dependencies. In daily stand-ups, I share testing progress and blockers. In retrospectives, I propose improvements like implementing visual regression testing. [Ceremonies] My value is ensuring quality is built in from the start—I've helped reduce production bugs by 60% through continuous testing. [Value] For example, in one sprint, I identified a testability issue during planning that would have blocked testing at the end. We adjusted the story to make it testable, avoiding a last-minute scramble. [Example]"

### Template: Describing Distributed Team Collaboration

1. **Challenge** (1 sentence): What's the difficulty?
2. **Strategies** (3-4 points): How do you handle it?
3. **Tools** (1-2 sentences): What tools enable async work?
4. **Result** (1 sentence): What's the outcome?

**Example:**
"Working with teams across the US, Europe, and India presents time zone challenges. [Challenge] I handle this by: identifying overlap hours for critical meetings, using async communication via Slack and Confluence, recording meetings for those who can't attend, and maintaining comprehensive documentation. [Strategies] Slack is for quick updates, Confluence for long-term reference, and Jira for work tracking—everyone has visibility regardless of time zone. [Tools] This approach enables seamless collaboration—we delivered 10 consecutive sprints on time despite an 11-hour time difference. [Result]"
